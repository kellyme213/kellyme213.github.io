Experimental Game Design

Experimental Game Design is an upper level studio arts course focusing on the creation of innovative, workable game prototypes using a variety of interactive multimedia. Games are considered as a new genre and are analyzed as cultural artifacts. The aesthetics of game design including character development, level design, game play experience, and delivery systems are covered. Flow, game theory, and game play gestalt are considered. Alternate gaming paradigms and emerging forms are encouraged.

Game Architecture

Digital Filmmaking

Students who take this class learn to make videos using lightweight video cameras, tripods, portable lighting kits, and a variety of microphones. They will leave the class understanding basic rules of composition for individual shots, basic sound recording for film and video, as well as the language of cinema editing including styles ranging from continuity to montage. The students will understand and be able to use multi-track video editing software and will be able to create dynamic relationships between sound and image. The student will know pre-production skills including basic scripting and storyboarding. Students will develop their critical capacity relative to analyzing cinema, video art, and emerging time-based media, and will acquire a basic knowledge of film history dating from the 1890's to the present. Students will also enhance their ability to work in teams on media projects and will learn about contemporary media practices and distribution.
