Spring 2020 Courses

Operating Systems

Discussion of various aspects of computer operating systems design and implementation. Topics include I/O programming, concurrent processes and synchronization problems, process management and scheduling of processes, virtual memory management, device management, file systems, deadlock problems, system calls, and interprocess communication.

Animation Production

Animation Production is designed to give an animation industry experience by completing the entire process of creating an animated short. Learning long-term time management and finding the key time cost/benefits will be essential to success. The course schedule is setup to replicate the industry model of frequent updates and feedback.

3D Visual Effects

An introduction to the technical techniques and principles of computer animation with a focus on rigging, skinning, character fx, particle simulations, compositioning and rendering. Lectures, discussion, and exposure to contemporary work enable students to develop skills in this rapidly evolving field.

Virtual Reality

This will be studio projects course, in which we'll develop VR experiences including games, immersive artworks, visualizations, learning simulations, or other applications of VR. Platforms include the CAVE room-scale VR system at the Emergent Reality Lab at the Tech Park as well as HMD systems such as the Vive and Oculus Rift. We'll look at the history of VR and related experiences, aesthetics, and theories of space, embodiment, interactivity, and immersion, and at engineering and computer science problems in VR technologies. This is an interdisciplinary class, for programmers, artists, architects, engineers, cognitive scientists, writers, musicians, and anyone interested in exploring virtual reality.

Game AI

This course introduces students to basic concepts and methods of artificial intelligence and their applications in computer games. The topics include decision making, movement, path finding, and AI for human-like characters.

3D Animation

Experimental Game Design

Experimental Game Design is an upper level studio arts course focusing on the creation of innovative, workable game prototypes using a variety of interactive multimedia. Games are considered as a new genre and are analyzed as cultural artifacts. The aesthetics of game design including character development, level design, game play experience, and delivery systems are covered. Flow, game theory, and game play gestalt are considered. Alternate gaming paradigms and emerging forms are encouraged.

Game Architecture

This is a class on the composition of game engines, the substrate between hardware and scripting. Topics include splines, collision detection, event management, shader programming, scene graph manipulations, and rigid body dynamics.

Digital Filmmaking

Students who take this class learn to make videos using lightweight video cameras, tripods, portable lighting kits, and a variety of microphones. They will leave the class understanding basic rules of composition for individual shots, basic sound recording for film and video, as well as the language of cinema editing including styles ranging from continuity to montage. The students will understand and be able to use multi-track video editing software and will be able to create dynamic relationships between sound and image. The student will know pre-production skills including basic scripting and storyboarding. Students will develop their critical capacity relative to analyzing cinema, video art, and emerging time-based media, and will acquire a basic knowledge of film history dating from the 1890's to the present. Students will also enhance their ability to work in teams on media projects and will learn about contemporary media practices and distribution.
