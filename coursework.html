Advanced Computer Graphics

This course will survey classic papers and current research in computer graphics. Topics include: advanced ray tracing, global illumination, photon mapping, subsurface scattering, mesh generation and simplification, subdivision surfaces, volumetric modeling, procedural modeling and texturing, weathering, physical simulation, appearance models.

Game Architecture

This is a class on the composition of game engines, the substrate between hardware and scripting. Topics include splines, collision detection, event management, shader programming, scene graph manipulations, and rigid body dynamics.

Game Development 2

Students work in interdisciplinary teams to create one large-scale 3D game, working from concept to public release. Projects may include games, educational games, serious games and simulations, and interactive artworks, and will focus on creative design, technical execution, and use of game design principles.

Virtual Reality Projects

In this project course, we will work with contemporary VR technologies including the Oculus Rift, the HTC Vive and the CAVE. Projects will be open ended, can be team-based, and may take many forms including artworks, performances, games, educational simulations, or visualization/sonification. Readings, screenings, lectures and discussions will explore virtuality and immersion from the perspectives of philosophy, theory, technology and user experience.
