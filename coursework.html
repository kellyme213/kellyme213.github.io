Operating Systems

Discussion of various aspects of computer operating systems design and implementation. Topics include I/O programming, concurrent processes and synchronization problems, process management and scheduling of processes, virtual memory management, device management, file systems, deadlock problems, system calls, and interprocess communication.

Animation Production

Animation Production is designed to give the student the experience of going through the entire process of creating an animated short. Learning long-term time management and finding the key time cost/benefits will be essential to success. The course schedule is setup to replicate the industry model of frequent updates and feedback.

3D Visual Effects

This course will explore conceptual ideas with a developed toolkit of skills. Skills possibly covered will be: scripting, advanced rendering and lighting, visual effects, fluids, and simulations. Emphasis will be placed on self-directed, conceptual, finished, high quality artwork. This course will be taught in an atelier style, where student motivation and collegial support are strongly emphasized.

Virtual Reality

This course explores the creation of experiences for virtual reality, including games, artworks, visualizations, and other applications. Students will develop VR projects for the CAVE, a multi-screen projection VR room, and for other consumer VR devices. The course will include screenings, readings, and demos surveying VR history, theory, and applications. In addition to developing projects using VR, the course will cover the basics of the design and construction of VR systems, and will gain hands-on experience with configuring and installing components in a CAVE system.

Fun with Cameras, Maya, and AR

This independent study will involve creating an AR app and Maya plugin. Users of the app will model a scene in Maya, export the model and import it in the AR app. Then, the user will display the model in augmented reality and record the position and orientation of the phone as it moves around in 3D space. Then, the app will export camera position data, and the Maya plugin will import the data.

Experimental Game Design

Experimental Game Design is an upper level studio arts course focusing on the creation of innovative, workable game prototypes using a variety of interactive multimedia. Games are considered as a new genre and are analyzed as cultural artifacts. The aesthetics of game design including character development, level design, game play experience, and delivery systems are covered. Flow, game theory, and game play gestalt are considered. Alternate gaming paradigms and emerging forms are encouraged.

A course exploring methods for 3D computer lighting, rendering, and camera authoring. We will discuss strong foundational theory from film in regards to lighting and cinematography, The course will focus on producing high fidelity rendered images for animation, games, and digital art.

Advanced Computer Graphics

This course will survey classic papers and current research in computer graphics. Topics include: advanced ray tracing, global illumination, photon mapping, subsurface scattering, mesh generation and simplification, subdivision surfaces, volumetric modeling, procedural modeling and texturing, weathering, physical simulation, appearance models.

Digital Filmmaking

Students who take this class learn to make videos using lightweight video cameras, tripods, portable lighting kits, and a variety of microphones. They will leave the class understanding basic rules of composition for individual shots, basic sound recording for film and video, as well as the language of cinema editing including styles ranging from continuity to montage. The students will understand and be able to use multi-track video editing software and will be able to create dynamic relationships between sound and image. The student will know pre-production skills including basic scripting and storyboarding. Students will develop their critical capacity relative to analyzing cinema, video art, and emerging time-based media, and will acquire a basic knowledge of film history dating from the 1890's to the present. Students will also enhance their ability to work in teams on media projects and will learn about contemporary media practices and distribution.
